## Slide 1.5 — The AI world: a family, not one thing
Visual: taxonomy — rules ⚙️ / recognition 👁️ / recommenders 🎯 / generative ✨

**Speaker notes:** Before "what is ChatGPT" — zoom out. AI isn't one thing; it's a family of very different machines, each built and working differently: (1) classic software — the computer follows an exact recipe someone wrote: calculator, traffic light, Excel; zero learning. (2) machine learning / recognition — learns patterns from millions of examples: face unlock on your phone, spam filter, Waze predicting traffic. (3) recommendation engines — learn what you liked, guess what you'll want: Netflix, YouTube. (4) generative AI — doesn't just recognize, CREATES new content: text, images, voice, video. Tonight's star is the text-creating kind: the LLM.

## Slide 2 — What is an LLM? (the 2-minute version)
Visual: a sentence with the last word missing → "The capital of France is ___"

**Speaker notes:** It's a program trained on enormous amounts of text to predict the next word — and that turns out to be enough to write, summarize, translate, plan, reason. No magic, no consciousness. Key implication to plant NOW (pays off in the trust moment later): *it always produces an answer that sounds right — sounding right and being right are not the same thing.*

## Slide 9 — ⚠️ Two rules (trust moment)
**Rule 1: It sounds confident even when it's wrong.**
**Rule 2: Don't paste anything you'd t want leaving your office.**

**Speaker notes:** THE 5-minute trust segment — deliver inside Demo 2 while a file is on screen. Show the live hallucination example (demo-script.md, Demo 2b): let it confidently get something wrong, let the room see it. ## Slide 10 — Upgrade 3: It can work FOR you
Visual: folder icon "Projects" + clock icon "Tasks"

**Speaker notes:** → **Demo 3** (live). Project = a dedicated room for one topic: its own instructions, its own files, all chats together. Set up once, every conversation starts smart. Task = ChatGPT acts on a schedule, ALONE — every morning, every Sunday — and the result waits for you. This is the moment it stops being a search engine.

## Slide 10.5 — Bonus: Borrow other people's experts

**Speaker notes:** → **Demo 4** (live, see demo-script.md). You don't have to build everything yourself — 159,000+ ready-made "expert versions" of ChatGPT in the GPT Store, built by companies and verified builders.
